**mirror-descent-lean: Formal Proofs of Mirror Descent and Bregman Divergence Convergence in Lean 4**

Lean 4 formal proofs of mirror descent with Bregman divergences: the three-point identity, the per-step descent bound, and O(1/√K) averaged convergence.

**Zero sorry statements.** Standard axioms only (`propext`, `Classical.choice`, `Quot.sound`).

## Why it matters

Mirror descent is the geometry-aware generalisation of gradient descent. Instead of measuring distance with the Euclidean norm, it uses a *Bregman divergence* generated by a strongly convex mirror map φ — and by choosing φ to match the geometry of the feasible set, it achieves dimension-favourable rates that plain gradient descent cannot. Two choices recover the most important first-order methods as special cases:

- **φ = ½‖·‖²** recovers projected (sub)gradient descent — the Bregman divergence becomes ½‖x − y‖².
- **φ = Σ xᵢ ln xᵢ** (negative entropy) gives exponentiated gradient / multiplicative weights on the simplex — the Bregman divergence becomes the KL divergence.

This library machine-checks the full convergence theory once, over a general real inner product space, so that every such instantiation inherits a proof rather than re-deriving one.

## Setting

A real inner product space `E`, with a mirror map φ : E → ℝ differentiable with gradient ∇φ, strongly convex with modulus σ > 0 on a convex domain C. The **Bregman divergence** is

```
D_φ(x, y) = φ(x) − φ(y) − ⟨∇φ(y), x − y⟩
```

For an objective f with subgradient gₖ at xₖ and step size αₖ, the mirror descent update is given by the **Bregman projection optimality condition** (a variational inequality):

```
∀ u ∈ C:  ⟨∇φ(x_{k+1}) − ∇φ(x_k) + α_k·g_k, u − x_{k+1}⟩ ≥ 0
```

which subsumes both the unconstrained update ∇φ(x_{k+1}) = ∇φ(x_k) − α_k·g_k and the constrained Bregman projection onto C.

## Key results

- **Three-point identity:** D_φ(x,z) = D_φ(x,y) + D_φ(y,z) + ⟨∇φ(y) − ∇φ(z), x − y⟩.
- **Per-step bound:**
  ```
  f(x_k) − f(x*) ≤ (D_φ(x*, x_k) − D_φ(x*, x_{k+1})) / α_k + α_k/(2σ)·‖g_k‖²
  ```
- **O(1/√K) averaged convergence:** with the optimal constant step α = √(2σD₀/(G²K)), the averaged regret satisfies
  ```
  (1/K) Σ_k (f(x_k) − f(x*)) ≤ G·√(2D₀/(σK))
  ```
  where D₀ = D_φ(x*, x₀) and G bounds the subgradient norms.

## Design note

The mirror update is stated as the **Bregman projection optimality condition** — the variational inequality satisfied by the argmin of α_k⟨g_k, ·⟩ + D_φ(·, x_k) over C. This single formulation subsumes both the unconstrained case (where it reduces to ∇φ(x_{k+1}) = ∇φ(x_k) − α_k·g_k) and the constrained case (projection back onto C), so neither needs separate treatment. The whole development works over a general real Hilbert space via Mathlib's `HasGradientAt`, rather than being specialised to ℝⁿ; the first-order convexity conditions are bundled as hypotheses in `MirrorMap`, with their equivalence to standard convexity + differentiability left as a separate concern. Special cases (projected gradient descent, exponentiated gradient) are obtained by constructing the appropriate `MirrorMap` instance.
